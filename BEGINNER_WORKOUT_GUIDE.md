## 1. BODYWEIGHT SQUATS

### Exercise Overview
**Primary Targets:** Legs, Glutes, Core, Lower Back  
**Secondary:** Calves, Cardiovascular System  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Equipment:** None

### Anatomy & Benefits

â”œâ”€â”€ Strengthens bones and connective tissue
â”œâ”€â”€ Enhances cardiovascular endurance
â””â”€â”€ Activates largest muscle group in body

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Starting Position:**
```
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart (hip to shoulder width)
2. Toes slightly turned outward (5-10 degrees)
3. Arms extended straight forward at shoulder height
4. Chest up, shoulders back
5. Neutral spine (natural curve in lower back)
```

**Descent (Lowering):**
```
1. Inhale as you begin the movement
2. Push hips back as if sitting in a chair
3. Bend knees to approximately 90 degrees
4. Weight in heels, not toes
5. Chest stays upright throughout
6. Knees track over toes (don't cave inward)
7. Descend slowly (2-3 seconds)
```

**Ascent (Rising):**
```
1. Exhale as you drive through heels
2. Push body back to starting position
3. Maintain upright chest throughout
4. Don't lock out knees at top
5. Controlled movement (not explosive)
```

### Form Cues & Corrections

| Common Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Knees caving inward | Quad/knee injury risk | Push knees outward, engage glutes |
| Chest leaning forward | Lower back strain | Keep chest up, push hips back |
| Heels lifting | Loss of power, ankle strain | Shift weight to heels, reduce depth if needed |
| Too fast | Poor form, momentum | Slow down, focus on control |
| Knees past toes | Patellofemoral pain | This is normal! Move hips back more |

### Progression Levels

**Level 1: Assisted Squat (Weeks 1-2)**
```
- Hold onto chair, counter, or door frame
- Reduces body weight resistance
- Builds confidence and form
- 2 sets of 10-12 reps
- Rest: 60 seconds between sets
```

**Level 2: Bodyweight Squat (Weeks 3-8)**
```
- Full bodyweight squat
- No support needed
- 3 sets of 12-15 reps
- Rest: 60 seconds between sets
- Tempo: 2 seconds down, 1 second hold, 2 seconds up
```

**Level 3: Tempo Squat (Week 9+)**
```
- Increase time under tension
- 4 seconds down, 2 second pause, 2 seconds up
- 3 sets of 10-12 reps
- Rest: 90 seconds between sets
```

**Level 4: Squat Pulses**
```
- Full squat down, then pulse up and down 5-10 times
- 3 sets of 8 reps (each rep = squat + pulses)
- Rest: 90 seconds between sets
```

### Breathing Pattern
```
Inhale: During lowering phase
Hold: At bottom of squat (1 second)
Exhale: During rising phase
Rhythm: Never hold breath for more than 3 seconds
```

```

**Progressive Program:**
```

### Common Questions

**Q: Should my knees go past my toes?**  
A: Yes! This is a natural movement pattern and doesn't increase injury risk if you have good form.

**Q: Why am I sore after squats?**  
A: DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness) is normal in first 2 weeks. Gradually subsides as muscles adapt.

**Q: Can I do squats every day?**  
A: Start with 3x/week. Advanced lifters can do 4-5x/week, but beginners need recovery.

**Q: How deep should I squat?**  
A: Start at 90 degrees (thighs parallel to floor). Deeper is okay if form is good.

â”œâ”€â”€ Improves sprint and jumping ability
â””â”€â”€ Increases metabolism via largest muscle activation

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Starting Position:**
```
1. Lie on back on floor or mat
2. Knees bent, feet flat on floor
3. Feet hip-width apart (about 10-12 inches)
4. Feet parallel (toes straight ahead)
5. Heels about 12 inches from glutes
6. Arms at sides, palms down (for stability)
7. Head on floor in neutral position
8. Core lightly engaged
```

**Hip Extension (Lifting):**
```
1. Exhale as you begin movement
2. Squeeze glutes hard (imagine clenching)
3. Drive through heels
4. Lift hips up toward ceiling
5. Continue until body forms straight line
6. Shoulders, hips, and knees in alignment
7. At top, maximum glute contraction
8. Hold for 1-2 seconds at top
```

**Lowering:**
```
1. Inhale as you lower
2. Controlled descent (2-3 seconds)
3. Lower hips to floor (but don't relax)
4. Tap floor lightly, immediately go back up
5. Maintain constant glute tension
```

### Form Cues & Corrections

| Common Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Hips not fully extended | Reduced glute activation | Squeeze harder, lift hips higher |
| Using lower back instead of glutes | Lower back strain | Focus on glute squeeze, use more leg drive |
| Knees caving inward | Knee injury risk | Push knees outward, engage glutes |
| Not feeling glute activation | Wrong muscles working | Pause 2 seconds at top, concentrate on squeeze |
| Feet too far from glutes | Reduced range of motion | Move feet 8-12 inches from glutes |
| Going too fast | Reduced muscle activation | Slow down, focus on 1-second pause at top |

### Progression Levels

**Level 1: Single-Leg Assisted (Weeks 1-2)**
```
- Lie on back, one knee bent, other leg extended or bent
- Hands on chest or at sides
- Lift hips using mostly one leg
- 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
- Rest: 60 seconds between sets
- Easier to learn glute activation
```

**Level 2: Basic Glute Bridge (Weeks 3-4)**
```
- Both feet on ground
- Standard bodyweight bridge
- 3 sets of 12 reps
- Hold 1 second at top
- Rest: 60 seconds between sets
```

**Level 3: Glute Bridge with Pause (Weeks 5-6)**
```
- Both feet on ground
- Hold 2-3 seconds at top
- Intense glute contraction
- 3 sets of 10 reps
- Rest: 90 seconds between sets
```

**Level 4: Single-Leg Glute Bridge (Week 7+)**
```
- Lift one leg while other drives bridge
- Significantly more challenging
- 3 sets of 8-10 reps per leg
- Rest: 90 seconds between sets
```

**Level 5: Advanced Variations**
```
Option A: Marching Glute Bridge
- Lift one foot 1 inch off ground at top
- Maintain hip level and height
- 3 sets of 10 per leg

Option B: Pulse Bridges
- At top, pulse hips up 10-15 times
- Then lower
- 3 sets of 8 pulses

Option C: Bulgarian Glute Bridge (requires elevated surface)
- One foot elevated on couch/chair
- Other leg drives
- 3 sets of 10 per leg

â”œâ”€â”€ Builds functional movement patterns
â””â”€â”€ Great for sports and daily activities

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Starting Position:**
```
1. Stand upright, feet hip-width apart
2. Arms at sides or across chest
3. Core engaged lightly
4. Shoulders back, chest up
5. Look straight ahead
```

**Forward Lunge (Forward Leg):**
```
1. Take a large step forward with right leg
2. Step should be 2-3 feet in front
3. Plant heel first, then roll to toes
4. Bend front knee to ~90 degrees
5. Front knee should track over ankle
6. Lower body until back knee nearly touches floor
7. Back heel should lift off ground
```

**Forward Lunge (Back Leg):**
```
1. Back knee lowers toward floor
2. Back knee bends to ~90 degrees
3. Back leg stays stable and strong
4. Front shin remains relatively vertical
5. Torso stays upright (don't lean forward)
```

**Return to Start:**
```
1. Push through front heel
2. Drive back leg forward to return to standing
3. Return to starting position
4. Immediately step forward with left leg
5. Alternate legs for set duration
```

### Form Cues & Corrections

| Common Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Front knee caving inward | Knee injury risk, quad pain | Push knee outward, engage glutes |
| Front knee past toes excessively | Kneecap stress | This is okay in moderation, but take longer step |
| Torso leaning forward | Reduced leg work, back strain | Keep chest upright, look straight ahead |
| Back knee slamming floor | Joint impact, not controlled | Lower slowly and under control |
| Narrow stance (not enough forward step) | Reduced range, less effective | Take bigger step forward |
| Looking down | Neck strain, loss of balance | Keep eyes forward on horizon |

### Progression Levels

**Level 1: Assisted Lunges (Weeks 1-2)**
```
- Hold onto chair, counter, or wall
- Reduced instability, focus on form
- 2 sets of 8 reps per leg
- Rest: 90 seconds between sets
- Learn movement pattern safely
```

**Level 2: Bodyweight Lunges (Weeks 3-4)**
```
- No support, arms at sides or crossed
- 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
- Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
```

**Level 3: Reverse Lunges (Weeks 5-6)**
```
- Step backward instead of forward
- Often feels more stable and less knee stress
- 3 sets of 12 reps per leg
- Rest: 60 seconds between sets
- Alternative: Do forward AND reverse each workout
```

**Level 4: Walking Lunges (Week 7+)**
```
- Continuous forward movement
- Step forward continuously
- 3 sets of 20 meters or 16 steps
- Rest: 2 minutes between sets
- More challenging balance and stability
```

**Level 5: Advanced Variations**
```
Option A: Lateral Lunges (sideways)
- Step to side, lunge to one direction
- 3 sets of 10 per side

Option B: Stationary Lunges
- Hold lunge position, pulse up and down
- 3 sets of 10 pulses

Option C: Jump Lunges
- Forward lunge with explosive jump at bottom
